Flood Safety Guidelines

Essential information and preparedness tips to help you and your family stay safe during flood emergencies.

Safety Tips

  • Stay informed through official alerts and updates.
  • Prepare an emergency kit with essentials.
  • Know your evacuation routes and shelters.
  • Secure important documents and valuables.
  • Stay away from floodwaters and do not attempt to cross flooded roads.

Before the Flood

  • Prepare an emergency kit with food, water, medicine, flashlight, and batteries
  • Keep important documents in waterproof containers
  • Know your evacuation routes and nearest evacuation centers
  • Monitor weather forecasts and flood alerts regularly
  • Secure outdoor items that could be swept away
  • Store drinking water in clean containers

During the Flood

  • Move to higher ground immediately if flooding begins
  • Avoid walking or driving through floodwater
  • Turn off electricity and gas if instructed to evacuate
  • Stay away from power lines and electrical wires
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts for updates
  • Do not attempt to swim through flowing water

After the Flood

  • Wait for authorities to declare the area safe before returning
  • Avoid floodwater as it may be contaminated
  • Check for structural damage before entering buildings
  • Throw away food that came in contact with floodwater
  • Document damage for insurance claims with photos
  • Watch for displaced wildlife, especially snakes

Emergency Kit Essentials

3-day supply of food and water First aid kit and medications Flashlight and extra batteries Battery-powered or hand-crank radio Whistle for signaling help Dust masks and plastic sheeting Multipurpose tool Personal hygiene items Copies of important documents Mobile phone with chargers and backup battery

Stay Informed

Follow official sources for weather updates and alerts. Download the PAGASA mobile app or tune in to local news stations. Enable emergency alerts on your mobile phone to receive critical notifications.
